AXIS CUSTOM APPAREL
CO-CREATOR | LEAD DESIGNER

One of the few spots with no "4-on-the-floor," spots on 2nd Street.

Armed with a killer jukebox, amazing bands that'd regulary pitstop after Philly or NYC for an off tour gig and a super, pseudo-seedy vibe.

Whatever.

One of my co-workers fiancés was in an old punk band called "The Virus" and was currently in a "pick-up band" featuring the former sit-in drummer from Dinosaur JR.

He liked my clever nonsense in bar advertising for events, specials, bands, etc. and asked if I'd be interested in being a part of "something sweet."

About 2 months later, with a direct-to-garment printer and a crazy idea, our brick-and-morter store was born.

I utilized my skater/street artist friends to canvass the town when they were out with our material. In return, I'd take a photo of their street art and bake them up some shirts the next day.

Pretty rad trade-off.

I also realized I love that entrepeneur thing.

CAPITAL CITY PROMOTIONS
CO-CREATOR | EMCEE | DESIGNER

Spinning off of our T-Shirt shop and utilizing the connections with the local art and music scene, we decided to try our hand at a production company.

Armed with a creative writing/journalist and top-notch schmoozer, yours truly with the silver tongue, outside-of-the-box ideas and versatile design skills and tastes, and the rockstar with the music hookups, charm for days, and seemed to know every single person in the city, Capital City Underground Promotions was born.

Partnering with amazing local companies out of Philly, DC, Baltimore, Harrisburg and NYC, we put on some of the biggest and best shows in the Capital City area.

We'd put our amazing local bands as opening acts for exposure. We featured the Suicide Girls, Vans Clothing/Shoes, Levi's, Converse, etc.

Not bad for 3 dudes and an idea. (We were pretty surprised that we actually pulled it off.)

Between juggling the shop, the 24/7 attention needed for promoting as well as trying to have some sort of personal life, it proved too much.

We closed it down after 8 months with one last blowout. That evening, we slept like we hadn't in a long time.

LIFELINE YOUTH SERVICES
YOUTH COORDINATOR

Working with kids ranks as one of the most challenging and fulfilling parts of my life.

I worked in tandem with my co-worker to supplement the outdated curriculum with pertinent information.

These boys ranged from age 14-18 and were on their last leg of placement before being out in the world. Some of them only knew Juvie centers.

We had to get them ready for the real world emotionally and inter-personally.

I would suppliment their individual programs and the curriculum with alternates that appealed to them if they were struggling.

I would teach literature and poetry through hip-hop which let them also know Shakespeare. We'd take that a step further and colaborate as a team creating a story and making it rhyme. Then I'd teach fractions through the beat we'd create together in Logic Pro or Abelton then move onto teaching frequencies and soundwaves, mathematics through EQing and much music theory throughout the process.

I genuinely loved and cared for that diverse group of guys.

I actually still hear from a couple of them all these years later.

That feels good.
